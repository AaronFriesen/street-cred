All About Reputation

General

Reputation is an indication of how a faction views you. Your standing with a particular faction affects how you can interact with that faction, and is influenced by performing quests and killing various mobs. Your standing with a particular faction is divided up in to the following levels:

  • Hated
  • Hostile
  • Unfriendly
  • Neutral
  • Friendly
  • Honored
  • Revered
  • Exalted

Gaining and Losing Reputation:

An in-game display of a player's
reputation with various factions.

The following are some of the ways to gain/lose reputation

  • Completing non-repeatable quests will increase your reputation with the quest giver’s faction
  • Mobs give full reputation
  • Mobs and quests sometimes increase your reputation
  • Most quests will give full reputation gain for that faction and one quarter of that reputation gain for the other related factions
  • At level 77 and above, characters can gain reputation with their faction’s races by completing quests for the Argent Tournament
  • There is no way to increase your reputation with factions fo the opposite team (Horde vs Alliance).
  • It is possible to lose reputation points with some factions by killing members of the faction or by assisting rival factions.
  • For rival factions, gaining reputation with one will normally decrease reputation with the other but at a greater rate

Rewards and Benefits:

Having a high reputation with a faction allows access to various rewards:

  • Gaining reputation with your home faction provides access to discounted prices at vendors
  • Gaining reputation with other factions may provide additional benefits, such as specific items, patterns, or spells that can be purchased from vendors
  • If you want a mount of a faction that is not your own, you must be Exalted with that factions

Managing Reputation:

There are several ways you can view your standing with a particular faction both in game and out of game.
Note: There is no way to view your reptation with factions of the other side (Horde vs Alliance).

In Game

You can check your reputation with all factions by pressing the “u” hotkey or by going to the Character window (“c” hotkey) and clicking on the Reputation tab along the bottom.

Out of Game

Reputation for all cities can also be checked on the Battle.net Armoury for characters that have reached level 10. For you convenience you can check your reputation with select factions from the Character Info page on this site.
